Why writing for money corrupted my intentions

Initially, I started blogging after a freeCodeCamp post on blogging led me to discover Hashnode. I have been programming for a few years, so I thought it was about time I started contributing what I had learned back into the community.

I published a few pieces on Hashnode before expanding to cross-post on Medium and Dev.to. I wrote about what I wanted to and learned a lot, so it was a great experience.

At first, I wanted to grow my audience. However, after a little while, I became absorbed in the Medium Partnership Program. Medium has a lot of articles with titles like Earn $100 a day in this one easy way or Grow your passive income stream by blogging (these are just examples, not anyone’s specific title). All the articles about creating passive income were intoxicating.

These articles led me down the rabbit hole, so I hopped on the follow-for-follow grind and made my way to one hundred followers.

After getting my first 100 followers, I joined the program and moved my stories behind a paywall. At first, I was excited and proud of my accomplishment.

I’ve been writing behind the paywall for two months. I’ve earned a few dollars.

Perhaps I’m not a great writer, or I’m not creating the correct type of content. I’m not sure. Regardless, I haven’t made a lot. The earnings have been poor, but worse, writing for money has corrupted my intentions. I started writing because I wanted to contribute what I’ve learned. It felt good because I imagined I was helping people.

When writing for money, I feel selfish. That has made the experience less enjoyable. So, I think it’s time I got back to the basics and started writing about what I want to.

If writing for passive income is your goal, then power to you. However, I want to get back to the basics and focus on writing what I want to write. I feel like I can better help people that way.

By Sean Coughlin on March 24, 2022.
