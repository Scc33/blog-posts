Why do titles matter

I apologize for the clickbait title, but it ties into my message. Having great content is not enough. Content must be connected to marketing to reach an audience.

A strong title is the best way to market your articles and draw in new readers. A great article with a bad title won’t have the same reach as an average article with a great title.

A good title has a few characteristics:

  1. First, it must describe what your content is about.
  2. Second, it should be catchy and memorable. One way of being catchy is through eliciting an emotional response.
  3. Finally, it should have keywords that will generate more searches. If you are unfamiliar with SEO, check out the article I wrote on it.

How to create a great title

Optimizing a title based on the three characteristics I mentioned is hard. Thankfully, there are great resources online that will help create titles. Of the online resources, Headline Studio is the best one that I’ve found.

Headline Studio by CoSchedule: Write Better Headlines
The Headline Analyzer that will score your overall headline quality and rate its ability to result in social shares…headlines.coschedule.com

Headline Studio grades titles based on a Headline Score and an SEO Score. It provides recommendations for improving titles and stores a version history for easy comparison of multiple options.

I’d recommend trying this tool out because it’s free, easy to use, and will help you write better titles.

Titles that I tested using Headline Studio while writing this article

While writing this article, I tried several different titles. Each iteration, I ran through the headline tool.

  • “How to Create Better Article Titles” = SEO score of 52
  • “How to Make a Better Article Title” = SEO score of 55
  • “How to Create Better Blog Post Titles” = SEO score of 68
  • “One Step to Create a Killer Blog Post Title” = SEO score of 77
  • “One Step to Create a Killer Blog Post Title (With Examples)” = SEO score of 74
  • “One Step to Create a Killer Blog Title (With Examples)” = SEO score of 59

Headline Studio is not a headline generator, so I had to rely on trial and error. I could make the title just a little bit better each time using the provided suggestions.

Headline Studio has been helpful for me. I hope it can help you too.
