How to Get Your First 1000 Views

Tips for new writers hoping to find success on Medium

I just broke through the 1,000 view marker this week. It’s a milestone that I didn’t imagine when I started writing. Over my new writer journey, these are the tips that have helped me.

Explore new and different topics

As a new writer, you haven’t found your niche yet. So far, I’ve written about technology, economics, tutorials, and writing on Medium because I like all those topics. In the future, I might explore writing about other topics.

Writing about new subjects is fun! It gives a perspective on what readers are interested in and can be helpful for building skills. Across my articles, I’ve seen a large performance gap. My best-performing articles have been tutorials and about my experience writing.

Write a lot

The trope on Medium is to write an article a day. I think that is overkill for the average individual. Writing a good article is time-consuming and mentally exhausting. In my book, committing to write a story a day is the road to quitting within a week.

I don’t think new writers should rush out on a story a day. I would focus on creating one or two good pieces a week. Over a year that adds up to between 50 and 100 stories! That’s a lot of content!

Another way of saying that is commitment is more important than velocity.

I tried to set out with a story-a-day pace. I found it exhausting and tedious. Since reducing my output, I believe my quality and satisfaction have increased. That leads me to my final tip.

Find Enjoyment in your Work

Have fun! Yeah, Medium is a tool to make money and find an audience. However, if you enjoy writing you will create better content and find more readers.

Best of luck!
