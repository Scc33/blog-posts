Starting A New Job is Hard

Change, while part of life, is tough.

Over the past few weeks, I graduated from college and started my first full-time job. I’m fortunate to have a great job fresh out of college, but all the changes have been more difficult to acclimate to than I expected.

Upon starting the role, I’ve noticed feeling uncomfortable and unproductive. Similar to difficult classes in school, much of the dialogue has gone over my head which is frustrating.

So, as a young person, Google is my first resource to check to see if this is normal. Through Google, I came across an article in the Harvard Business Review that deeply resonated.

Why Starting a New Job Feels So Awkward
Starting at a new job in a new workplace is exciting, but it can also be uncomfortable. Regardless of how many jobs…hbr.org

The article explains that there are psychological reasons why starting a new job is hard. It lists three main challenges:

  1. “Your prediction engine fails.”
  2. “You don’t know the language.”
  3. “You don’t have a squad — yet.”

All of these challenges scream being the new person. However, what I’ve seen is that these hardships come from change.

As I’ve grown up, I’ve felt change consistently. It is part of the human condition. While humans are built to adapt and learn, the process isn’t always easy or fun. It takes time and dedication to learn and grow.

I hope to look back upon all the changes in my life to see that I grew and become a better person because of them.
