A few months ago, I wrote an article about becoming a consistent blogger. That article became my most popular piece. So in the form of the best Hollywood studios, I’m creating a sequel. How does one become a more consistent blogger?

Start by finding what motivates you to write

I first started reading blog posts years ago. Initially, I wasn’t considering becoming a blogger myself. Over time, I started developing an itch to create new content. The desire to create original works eventually became an irresistible itch for me.

Once I knew I wanted to write, I tried to sit down and become a writer. After multiple tries, I never got anywhere. This leads to my next point.

Find what holds you back from writing

My problem was never initiative. I could easily find inspiration and start, but I never was able to find the desire to carry through on my goals. I learned that staying consistent is hard! It takes more dedication than I ever expected, so willpower alone won’t be enough.

It took trial and error, but eventually, I found that I was too easily distracted. So I started to create constant reminders and remove barriers to writing. And it worked.

Take action to remove the barriers blocking you from writing

I’ve followed three key tenets to stay consistent with writing. These ideas have been working for me, and I would recommend every blogger give them a try.

1) Create reminders for yourself:

  • Take a sticky note and put it on your bathroom mirror.
  • Have a reminder or alarm on your phone trigger every day.
  • Ask a friend to annoy you about it.
  • Change your desktop image to say “write”

2) Remove barriers to blogging:

  • Keep a journal of ideas.
  • Read, write, read. Every time you read, write a little response.
  • Never close Medium on your computer.
  • Download the Medium app to your phone and set up notifications.

3) Try to stay motivated:

  • Track your stats.
  • Seek out constructive criticism to grow and find positive feedback to feel good about your progress.
  • Publicly commit to friends, family, and the internet that you will publish

In addition to what works for me, try to find your own ways of creating constant reminders and removing existing barriers. Best of luck, and keep writing.
