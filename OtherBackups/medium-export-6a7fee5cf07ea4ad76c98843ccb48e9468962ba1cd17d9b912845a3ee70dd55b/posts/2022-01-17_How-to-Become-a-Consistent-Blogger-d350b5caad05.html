How to Become a Consistent Blogger

Warning: it is easier than you think

I first started reading blog posts years ago. Initially, I didn’t consider becoming a blogger myself. However, the desire to create something original becomes an irresistible itch.

The community seemed so warm and welcoming so I often tried to dip my toes into become a content creator. Each time I tried to start my own writing, I would fail. My road to publishing any content is littered with my half started stories and unfinished drafts.

My problem wasn’t initiative. I could easily find an inspiration and start, but I never was able to find the desire to carry through on my goals. I learned that staying consistent is hard! It takes more dedication than I ever expected.

I thought that I just didn’t want it bad enough or eventually I would find more dedication. However, distractions always exist and something would always draw my attention away.

Recently, I took up the pen again. This time I tried something different besides just my will power. I started to create constant reminders and remove barriers to writing. And it worked.

After years of half-starts, I actually wrote and released my first article. And I have no plans of stopping. This time feels more real and maintainable. I owe my current success to the reminders and lowered barries.

These ideas have been working for me and I would recommend every blogger give them a try.

Ideas for creating blogging reminders:

  • Take a sticky note and put it on your bathroom mirror.
  • Have a reminder or alarm on your phone trigger everyday.
  • Ask a friend to annoy you about it.

Ideas to remove barriers to blogging:

  • Keep a journal of ideas.
  • Read, write, read. Everytime you read write a little response.
  • Never close Medium on your computer.

Find ways that you can create constant reminders for yourself and remove existing barriers. Best of luck and on keep writing.
